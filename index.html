A brief history of IoT

The Internet of Things (IoT) essentially refers to an ecosystem of discrete computing devices with sensors connected through the infrastructure of the internet. The concept may have been bubbling away in the industry for a long time, but the democratisation of computing technology through the availability and affordability of small computing devices has now pushed it firmly into the mainstream.

So how did we get to this point?

A brief trawl through the IoT’s evolution can be summed up quite simply as things getting smaller – a cycle of minaturisation that began with computers the size of a room and ended up with a smartphone in the palm of hands.
